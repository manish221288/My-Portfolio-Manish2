About Me

I am a computational biophysicist with over 5 years of postdoctoral research experience under the guidance of Prof. Florence TAMA at ITbM, Nagoya University, Japan. I earned my Ph.D. in structural bioinformatics in 2019 under Prof. D. Velmurugan at the University of Madras, Chennai, India.

My research focuses on computational drug design, structural modeling, and molecular dynamics simulations to advance our understanding of protein-ligand complexes for drug development. I have extensive experience in integrating AI-driven techniques with traditional computational methodologies to accelerate drug discovery.

Currently, I lead R&D efforts at Nuvo AI, working on AI-based drug discovery platforms and antibody design against cancer. My work spans multiple domains including circadian clock proteins, membrane biophysics, and multidrug resistance transporters.

NIR Fluorogenic Probes
